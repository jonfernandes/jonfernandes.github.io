1.   Customers reacting to policy change 
 

Please focus on these positives
·         We have listened to our customers’ feedback and have worked carefully to improve their experience.
 
·         We changed our policy to allow more options for bikes than before – we can now take bikes in a box in order to try and transport more but have also retained space for a fully assembled bikes.
 
·         We kept the flexibility of pre-booked and same-day services now for boxed and fully mounted bikes.
 
·         For those using a box, we provide a box for you if you need one (and the tools / space to work in).
 
·         Our box option is even safer – bikes are better protected and tamper-free.
 
·         We will use our space on board more efficiently so we can look to accept even more bikes because we value our cyclists.

·         We accept any size single bike box (unfortunately no double boxes or tandems).  Avoid the term “normal-sized bike”.
 
 
Messages to Avoid
 
·         Anything about dimensions and set types.  This caused the uproar in the first place online.  We accept all single bike boxes, period!
 
·         Anything about mixed fleet.  This is an internal message only.
 
·         Don’t say we will dismantle the bikes for customers, we are trained experts etc.  This may have very bad implications legally.  Our teams are there to provide great customer service and assist where possible with making the experience more seamless.

3. Bikes – Basic FAQs
 
1.     How much does it cost?

•    A bike costs £30 if you book in advance to guarantee it on your train, and £25 on the day if you just turn up with it, not guaranteed on your train.  

•    Normally Eurodespatch will get the bike on the passengers train, otherwise it will usually travel on one of the next 3 trains.  The small print says within 24 hour but in reality it very rarely happens.

2.     Where can bikes be registered?
 
SPI (Eurodespatch)  - drop off desk at check-in.
 
 PNO (Géoparts) - drop off desk at check-in
 
 BXS (SNCB Baggage office) – located to the left of check-in Hall as you are facing it.
 
Lille (City One) – in the ‘Espace Voyageurs Handicapés’ next to SNCF ticket office near approximately 100m before Eurostar check-in.
 
3. What’s the rule on foldable bikes?

Foldable bikes in a case under 85cm can still travel as one of the 2 items of luggage on board on ANY EUROSTAR ROUTE OR TRAIN.  They must be inside the case to be able to check in.
 
4. Is it possible to transport tandems?  

No, and it never has been on Eurostar.
 
 
 
 

4. Fully Assembled Bikes: FAQs
 
1.    Why did you try to stop accepting them and then re-introduce them?
  • When we changed our policy and asked passengers to put their bikes in a bag or box, we wanted to make it simple for customers, to protect the bikes and ultimately to be able to carry more bikes.
  • We are very constrained on space on our trains as passengers are increasingly bringing more and more registered luggage.
  • We understand that this was unpopular with some of our customers so we have listened and worked to find a solution.
  • After careful thought and planning we have taken the decision to continue to allow fully mounted bikes on board but of course this can only be a small number because of the space constraints.
2. Will this revised policy apply to your new trains – will passengers be able to bring fully mounted bikes on board your new trains when they are introduced into service?

Yes – the policy will apply to all our trains.

3. How many fully mounted bikes can you take on board?

As we accept a combination of boxes & fully assembled bikes now this will vary from train to train, so for now we can’t put a number on it while we assess the demand from our customers.




5. BIKE BOX FAQs

1.     Do we need to allow extra time for dismantling?

Eurodespatch currently recommends 60minutes  before departure, this hasn’t changed.
2.     What are the box dimensions ? 

We accept all bike boxes that carry one single pedal bike - simple.
We do not accept double boxes or tandems.

4. How much dismantling is required? 

This depends entirely on the bike.  Both wheels, definitely.  Pedals, mostly yes, apart from the really small pro-type pedals that clip into cycle shoes.  

 5. Will mudguards and panniers racks need removing?

Yes, as they hang out over the wheels and will in most cases protrude too much to fit in a box. Panniers and racks have to be removed currently and this has not changed.

6. Is there a dedicated space available at the station for dismantling?

Yes, Eurosdespatch or its continental equivalents.

7. Will tools be available at the station?

Yes.  Tool kits will be provided.  These kits have anything you could need give or take, but basic dis-assembly needs a couple of Allen keys and isn’t complicated.

8. Will staff be at hand to assist? 

Please don’t give the impression that we will dismantle the bikes for customers, that we are trained experts or anything similar.  This may have consequences from a legal point of view. Our teams are there to provide great customer service and assist where possible with making the experience more seamless

9. Do we accept liability in case of damage/accidents following the assembly of bikes with staff assistance? 

This will all be on a case-by-case basis if it becomes a legal matter.  But in principle we do not accept liability, as with any other item of luggage.
We also expect bikes to be transported more safely under the protection of a box.

10. How long does it take to dismantle a bike?

6. What's showing on dotcom

In the helpcentre FAQs and the bike page on dotcom customers will find the below message:

Folding bicycles can be taken on any Eurostar train, as long as they’re smaller than 85cm when fully folded. Before going through check-in they’ll also need to be in a protective case that covers the whole bike.
 
All non-folding bikes or those over 85cm long will need to travel with our registered luggage service, Eurodespatch. This is available from London, Paris, Lille and Brussels, and we have a couple of options for you to choose from.
 
You can put your bike in a padded bike bag or bike box. We can provide a box for you, which is included in the service price and this is the best option for protecting your bike.
 
If you’d prefer to keep your bike fully assembled, Eurodespatch has a limited number of spaces per train, so you’ll need to call us for availability.
